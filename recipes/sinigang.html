Sinigang na Baboy

Description:

Sinigang na Baboy is a traditional Filipino soup known for its sour and savory taste, typically made with pork, tamarind, and a mix of vegetables. The unique tanginess comes from tamarind, but other souring agents like calamansi or green mango can also be used. It's a comforting and hearty dish enjoyed with rice, perfect for rainy days.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (belly or ribs), cut into chunks
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 2-3 tbsp tamarind paste or 1 packet sinigang mix (adjust to taste)
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 5-6 okra, whole or halved
  • 1 cup long green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch water spinach (kangkong) or spinach
  • 2 green chili peppers (optional, for extra heat)
  • Salt or fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • 6 cups water

Steps to Cook:

  1. Boil Pork: In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the pork, onion, and tomatoes, and let it boil until the pork is tender, about 45-60 minutes. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.
  2. Add Tamarind Paste and Seasoning: Add tamarind paste or sinigang mix to the pot. Stir well and adjust the sourness to your taste.
  3. Add Vegetables: Add the radish, okra, and green beans, cooking them until they are tender but still slightly crisp, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add Leafy Greens and Chili Peppers: Add the water spinach (kangkong) and green chili peppers (if using) to the pot. Let them cook for another 2-3 minutes until wilted.
  5. Adjust Seasoning and Serve: Season with salt or fish sauce to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.
